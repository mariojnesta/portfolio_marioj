CMIP6 Climate Model Ensemble Analysis

GEOG 434 — Climate Science — Spring 2025

A multi-model ensemble analysis comparing projected changes in temperature, precipitation, and soil moisture under low- and high-emissions pathways using Python, xarray, and CMIP6 data.

Full research paper available as PDF.

1. Research Question

Research Question & Hypothesis

State the central research question your analysis addresses. Example structure: "How do [variable(s)] differ between SSP1-2.6 and SSP3-7.0 scenarios across [region/period], and what are the implications for [outcome/policy domain]?"

State your hypothesis: what did you expect to find, based on prior literature or physical reasoning? Then describe whether results confirmed, complicated, or challenged that expectation.

2. Background

Literature Review & Background

Provide background on CMIP6 (Coupled Model Intercomparison Project Phase 6) and its role in IPCC assessment reports. Explain what SSP (Shared Socioeconomic Pathway) scenarios represent — particularly the contrast between SSP1-2.6 (aggressive mitigation) and SSP3-7.0 (high-emissions, fragmented world).

Discuss the prior literature on one or more of your focal variables (temperature, precipitation, soil moisture). What has been documented about projected changes? What uncertainty exists across model ensembles? Which regions or systems are most sensitive?

Situate your analysis within the broader context of climate science and, where relevant, the policy implications of diverging trajectories. Cite 3–6 key sources (IPCC reports, peer-reviewed articles, CMIP6 model documentation).

Suggested citations to integrate: O'Neill et al. (2016) on SSP scenarios; Eyring et al. (2016) on CMIP6; relevant IPCC AR6 Working Group I chapters; any additional sources from your course readings.

3. Data & Methods

Data Sources & Analytical Methods

Data

Describe the CMIP6 datasets you used: model names (e.g., CESM2, MPI-ESM1-2-HR), ensemble members (e.g., r1i1p1f1), time periods, spatial resolution, and the variables extracted (tas for temperature, pr for precipitation, mrso or similar for soil moisture). Note the data source (e.g., Earth System Grid Federation / ESGF).

Methods

Outline your analytical workflow: (1) data loading and inspection using xarray; (2) any preprocessing steps (unit conversion, regridding, time slicing); (3) ensemble averaging or spread calculation; (4) anomaly computation relative to a baseline period; (5) comparison between SSP1-2.6 and SSP3-7.0 outputs; (6) visualization in Matplotlib/Cartopy.

Note any analytical decisions you made — choice of baseline period, how you handled missing data, how ensemble uncertainty was characterized. Briefly discuss any limitations of the approach.

4. Analysis & Discussion

Analysis & Discussion

Temperature Projections

Describe your findings on projected temperature changes. How do SSP1-2.6 and SSP3-7.0 diverge by mid-century and end-of-century? What is the magnitude of the difference? Are there regional patterns of note? How does ensemble spread compare between scenarios?

Precipitation Projections

Describe your precipitation findings. Where do the two scenarios show the greatest divergence? Are there drying or wetting trends in specific regions? How confident are you in these signals given ensemble spread?

Soil Moisture Projections

Describe your soil moisture findings. How do they relate to the temperature and precipitation signals? What implications do projected changes have for agriculture, drought risk, or ecosystem function?

5. Conclusions

Conclusions

Summarize the key differences you found between SSP1-2.6 and SSP3-7.0 across the three variables. What is the most important takeaway about the consequences of diverging emissions pathways?

Discuss limitations: what aspects of uncertainty couldn't you fully characterize? What model biases or data gaps affected your analysis? How might results differ with additional ensemble members or higher-resolution data?

End with a forward-looking statement: what does this analysis imply for climate policy, adaptation planning, or future research directions? Connect the technical findings back to real-world significance.
