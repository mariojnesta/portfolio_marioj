LEGO Power T Usability Testing Report

ENGL 360 — Technical Writing

A formal usability testing report evaluating the assembly instructions for the UTK LEGO Power T kit. Demonstrates technical writing, user-centered research design, and evidence-based recommendations.

Overview

Project Overview

Describe the subject of the usability test: the UTK LEGO Power T kit — what it is, who assembles it, and what instructions it comes with. Why was this an interesting usability testing subject? What problem or gap in the existing instructions did you set out to investigate?

Describe the broader context of the assignment within ENGL 360: the goals of the usability testing report genre, the audience for your report, and the stakes of the evaluation (i.e., who would act on your recommendations and why it matters).

Methodology

Testing Methodology

Describe your research design: how many participants did you recruit, how did you select them (were they UTK students? fans? first-time LEGO builders?), and what protocol did you follow? Did you use think-aloud protocols? Observation checklists? Likert-scale surveys?

What specific tasks did participants complete? What did you measure or record — completion time, number of errors, points of confusion, verbal expressions of frustration or confidence? How did you analyze the data you collected?

Key Findings

Key Findings

Summarize the most significant finding: where did participants most frequently encounter difficulty, confusion, or error? What specific features of the instructions contributed to this problem — unclear diagrams, ambiguous step numbering, insufficient detail for a critical step?
