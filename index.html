About

Experiences at UTK

My time at UTK has been about stacking skills I didn't always know would connect. The geography and sustainability major gave me spatial analysis, climate science, and GIS. The policy minor taught me how decisions get made and how to think about tradeoffs. Courses like Maps, Society & Power (GEOG 421) changed how I read any map or dataset — I spent the semester studying how cartographic choices encode power, leading discussions on narrative cartography, and analyzing Cold War-era atlases as political tools. Meteorology (GEOG 334) grounded my atmospheric science knowledge in radar interpretation, skew-T analysis, and synoptic forecasting. And Climate Science (GEOG 434) pushed me into building a full CMIP6 ensemble analysis pipeline in Python. The through-line is that I'm drawn to work that's both technical and critical — I want to be good at handling the data and also thoughtful about what it means and who it serves.

Specialty Area & Preparation

My specialty is applied climate data analysis and cartographic design. On the data side, I work with Python, xarray, and NetCDF to analyze CMIP6 multi-model ensemble projections across emissions scenarios. On the mapping side, I use ArcGIS Pro and QGIS to create everything from analytical deliverables to creative cartographic projects — including a Great Smoky Mountains map styled after the Breath of the Wild map from The Legend of Zelda, with custom contour lines and points of interest. My meteorological training in radar, skew-T analysis, and synoptic forecasting rounds out the atmospheric science foundation, and my policy minor gives me a framework for connecting technical output to real decisions.

Future Goals

I'm aiming for an applied climate data role after graduation — something like a climate data analyst, meteorological technician, or research support position at a place like NOAA GFDL, Oak Ridge National Lab, or a National Weather Service office. Longer term, I'm interested in graduate school at the intersection of climate science and geography, and potentially operational meteorology. Everything in this portfolio — the modeling work, the GIS projects, the critical geography thinking, the policy background — is building toward that first step into professional climate work.
