About

🎓

Experiences at UTK

My time at UTK has spanned both the technical and the interpretive sides of geography. Courses like Maps, Society & Power gave me a framework for understanding how cartographic choices are never neutral — who draws the map, what gets named, and what gets left out are all political decisions. Meanwhile, coursework in Meteorology, Climate Science, and GIS has built my quantitative toolkit. The combination — critical spatial thinking backed by real analytical skill — is the through-line of my undergraduate education.

🗺️

Specialty Area & Preparation

My specialty sits at the intersection of applied climate data analysis and cartographic design. I've worked with CMIP6 multi-model ensembles in Python using xarray and NetCDF workflows, comparing emissions scenarios and analyzing temperature, precipitation, and soil moisture projections. On the cartography side, I work fluidly in both ArcGIS Pro and QGIS — including a project reimagining the Great Smoky Mountains as a Zelda-inspired map that required deep familiarity with contour styling and custom symbology. My Public Policy Analysis minor adds the policy and governance layer that makes this work matter beyond the technical.

🔭

Future Goals

After graduation in May 2027, I'm aiming for a role as a climate data analyst or meteorological technician — ideally in a research or applied government setting like NOAA's Geophysical Fluid Dynamics Laboratory, Oak Ridge National Laboratory, or a regional National Weather Service office. I'm also seriously weighing graduate programs in climate science, environmental data science, or physical geography. Every piece of work in this portfolio — the critical analysis, the GIS projects, the CMIP6 modeling — is a deliberate step toward that goal. I want to do work that is technically defensible and policy-relevant.
