Additional Items

Supplementary context on my policy minor, Spanish fluency, and broader technical skills.

Additional Item 1

Public Policy Analysis Minor

University of Tennessee, Knoxville — Baker School of Public Policy & Public Affairs

I'm minoring in Public Policy Analysis because I think technical work in climate and geography is only as useful as the decisions it informs. The minor has given me a framework for understanding how policy gets made, how institutions weigh tradeoffs, and how to communicate scientific findings to people who aren't scientists. It complements the geography major by connecting the data and spatial analysis work to the governance and decision-making side of environmental issues.

Additional Item 2

Spanish Language Fluency

I'm fluent in Spanish — it's a heritage language for me. In the context of geography and environmental work, that means I can engage directly with Spanish-speaking communities, access research and data published in Spanish, and work in fieldwork contexts across Latin America and the Caribbean without a language barrier. It's an asset I expect to use professionally, especially in federal agencies like NOAA and USGS that serve diverse populations.

Additional Item 3

Transferable Skills

Beyond my geography coursework, I've built skills in web development (React, Node.js, HTML/CSS), data visualization, and statistical modeling through personal projects. These aren't geography-specific but they're the same underlying skills — working with data, building things that communicate information clearly, and solving problems programmatically.
